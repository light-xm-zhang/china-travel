Entry Requirements

Visa-Free Entry to China in 2026

China has dramatically expanded its visa-free policies. As of 2026, passport holders from 50+ countries can enter without a visa for short stays.

✈️

240-Hour (10-Day) Transit

No visa needed

The former 144-hour policy was upgraded to 240 hours (10 days) in December 2024. You can now travel across 24 provinces during your transit stay.

Eligibility: Citizens of 54 countries including USA, UK, Canada, Australia, NZ, EU nations, Japan, South Korea, Brazil, and more.

Requirements:

  • Valid passport (3+ months validity)
  • Confirmed onward ticket to a third country within 240 hours
  • Complete a temporary entry form on arrival
🛂

30-Day Visa-Free Entry

Valid through Dec 31, 2026

China's unilateral visa waiver allows citizens from 50 countries to enter for up to 30 days for tourism, business, or family visits — no transit ticket needed.

Money Matters

Payment Setup — The Mobile-First Country

China is nearly cashless. Setting up mobile payment before you depart is the single most important thing you can do.

支

Alipay

#1 Recommended for Tourists

  • ✓ International version with full English interface — the most foreigner-friendly payment app
  • ✓ Link Visa, Mastercard, Amex, or JCB cards directly
  • ✓ Transactions under 200 RMB (~$28) are fee-free; 3% fee above that
  • ✓ One QR code for metro, buses, and ferries in all major cities
  • ✓ Built-in DiDi (ride-hailing), bike-sharing, food delivery, and ticket booking
  • 💡 Set up before departure — SMS verification can fail via international roaming
微

WeChat / WeChat Pay

The Everything App

  • ✓ Universal acceptance — even the smallest street vendors use WeChat Pay
  • ✓ Access to Dianping (restaurant reviews), mini-programs for attraction tickets, and more
  • ⚠ Foreigners sometimes face identity verification issues — set up Alipay as your primary
  • 💡 If you can get it working, it's the most powerful app for traveling in China

e-CNY (Digital Yuan) — Fee-Free Alternative

China's central bank digital currency app now supports anonymous wallets for short-term visitors. Zero service fees and works offline via NFC. A great backup.

Cash (RMB) — Still Accepted by Law

Since February 2026, all physical merchants must accept RMB cash by law. Carry 200–500 RMB in small bills as a backup for remote areas or dead batteries.

All-in-One

Internet Access & VPN

Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and many Western services are blocked in China. You have two main options:

📱

China eSIM (Recommended)

Activates with just your passport at the airport. No VPN needed for essential services. Providers: Airalo, Nomad, Holafly.

🔒

VPN (Install Before Departure)

Install and test a reliable VPN before you fly — VPN websites are often blocked in China. ExpressVPN and NordVPN are popular choices.

Quick Connectivity Checklist

  • ☑ Purchase and activate a China-compatible eSIM
  • ☑ Install and test VPN on all devices before departure
  • ☑ Download offline maps (Amap/Baidu/Apple Maps) for your cities
  • ☑ Download offline Chinese language packs in Google Translate
  • ☑ Save important addresses in Chinese characters (for taxi drivers)
Getting Around

Transportation in China

China's transportation infrastructure is world-class. Here is how to navigate it like a pro.

🚄

High-Speed Rail

The world's largest high-speed rail network connects all major cities at 300+ km/h. Book via Trip.com (English) or the official 12306 app (Chinese only).

  • • Beijing → Shanghai: ~4.5 hours
  • • Shanghai → Hangzhou: ~1 hour
  • • Chengdu → Xi'an: ~3.5 hours
  • • Book 2–14 days ahead for best seats
🚇

Metro Systems

Every major city has a clean, efficient, and English-signed metro. Pay with Alipay's transport QR code — no need for local transit cards.

  • • Single rides: 3–10 RMB ($0.40–1.40)
  • • All signs in Chinese + English
  • • Runs ~6:00 AM to ~11:00 PM
✈️

Domestic Flights

For long distances (Beijing → Guilin, Shanghai → Lijiang), flying saves time. Book on Trip.com with international cards.

  • • Major airlines: Air China, China Eastern, China Southern
  • • Arrive 2 hours early for domestic flights
  • • ID/passport required for check-in
